The ambiguity nobody warns you about

Singular potentials have a way of humbling you. You write down what looks like a perfectly reasonable regularization scheme, get a perfectly reasonable-looking answer, and then realize a colleague's equally reasonable scheme gives a different number. Nobody is wrong, exactly — the ambiguity is real, and it's been sitting quietly in non-perturbative dark matter calculations for longer than I've been doing this.

Most of this year has gone into a formalism that removes that ambiguity outright, rather than just picking a convention and hoping it generalizes. It's slower than I expected, and more satisfying than I expected, in roughly equal measure.

What a liquid argon detector teaches a theorist

I spent a stretch of my MSc years analysing raw detector data at ASTROCENT, working with C++ and ROOT instead of Mathematica. It recalibrated something in how I think about theory. A resonance on paper is a clean bump in a cross-section. A resonance in real data is buried under noise, cuts, and a dozen systematic effects that don't care how elegant your Hamiltonian is.

I don't do experimental work day to day anymore, but I try to keep that gap in mind whenever a calculation feels a little too tidy.

Sommerfeld enhancement, for the version of me who didn't get it yet

The short version: when dark matter particles are slow and feel a long-range force between them, their wavefunctions distort before they ever get close enough to annihilate. That distortion can boost the annihilation rate by orders of magnitude — which matters enormously for anything trying to infer particle physics from cosmological or astrophysical signals.

The longer version is most of my MSc thesis. I still think the short version is the one worth remembering first.

Starting a project with no thesis deadline attached

Every project I'd worked on before had a defense date somewhere on the horizon. This is the first one that doesn't, and it changes the texture of the work in a way I didn't fully anticipate — fewer artificial milestones, more of my own judgment about when a result is actually solid enough to write down.

New collaboration, new country's time zone to coordinate across, same stubborn coupled equations. So far, so good.
