Multi-Channel Dark Matter Scattering

With Dr. Ayuki Kamada, University of Warsaw. Extending single-channel scattering results to multi-channel systems with mass splittings between states — the regime where threshold effects and resonances actually start to matter. Part of this is physics, part of it is numerics: building stable methods for solving multi-state coupled Schrödinger systems that don't fall apart near a resonance. Manuscript in preparation.

jan 2026 — present
ongoing

Non-Perturbative Frameworks & Singular Solutions

Collaboration with Kavli IPMU, Japan. Singular potentials have long created ambiguities in non-perturbative dark matter calculations — different reasonable choices give different answers. This project resolves that ambiguity with a stable, unambiguous numerical formalism that generalizes across a wide class of dark matter models. Manuscript in preparation.

Quantum Theory of Dark Matter Scattering

A. Kamada, T. Kuwahara, A. Patel

Establishes a direct relation between dark matter scattering and annihilation rates through phase shifts and Sommerfeld enhancement in quantum scattering theory. Grew out of my MSc thesis, Numerical Study of New Forces of Dark Matter, advised by Dr. Ayuki Kamada.
